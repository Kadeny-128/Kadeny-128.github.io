# Books & Podcasts ⏰

My December was all about philosophy. I picked up the “Daily Stoic” by Ryan Holiday and his daily lessons completely change the way I perceive uncontrollable events. Then, I turned to study wider range of philosophy, that involves human history, religions, physics…

- “Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind” by Yuval Noah Harari
“Homo sapiens” refers to wise human, which is us. We often think that we are the only humans that are around, which is true, at least for the past 10,000 years. But there used to be other human species (the homo species group) than us. Nevertheless, only us, the Homo sapiens has survived the cruelty of nature and evolved into wise human beings in the current days. In this book, Yuval touched upon how the Homo sapiens (us) underwent Cognitive Revolution, that gains us the ability to believe and create fictional thoughts that do not tangibly exist in this world. You probably think that is no big deal, but let me explain to you how that ability single-handedly change the trajectory of our human species. Because fiction not only enabled Homo sapiens to imagine and speak of things, but to do so collectively. Large-scale cooperations, such as a state, a company, or a church, are based on common myths that exist only in people’s collective imaginations. These myths bring human species closer and allow them to work with each other: Catholics who have never met each other can work to build an orphanage together because they both believe in God. However, you can never convince a monkey to give up a banana in his hand, in return to go to monkey heaven and enjoy eternal peace.  “Wolves and chimpanzees cooperate far more flexibly than ants, but they can do so only with small numbers of other individuals that they know intimately. Sapiens can cooperate in extremely flexible ways with countless numbers of strangers. That’s why Sapiens rule the world, whereas ants eat our leftovers and chimps are locked up in zoos and research laboratories.”

Subscribed

- “Everything is F*cked” by Mark Manson
If you are tired of traditional self-help guides that only repeat the same idea over and over again, consider to check out Mark Manson’s philosophy! Mark comes from a counterintuitive perspective (some might find him passive-aggressive), and discusses hope as a fuel necessary to drive human beings forward. Let me share an interesting theory with you: Human has two brains, a thinking brain and a feeling brain. Thinking brain, obviously, is the impartial, accurate, rational brain; it is good at making calculations, but it also requires effort and energy. The feeling brain, on the other hand, operates fast and effortlessly; we tend to think it is inaccurate and irrational. Let’s pretend that they are on a car, the consciousness car. Our preconceived notion of how our brain works, is that the thinking brain is driving behind the wheels, while the feeling brain is whining and ranting on the passenger’s seat, an annoying distraction on the road. We assume that we are totally in control of our everything merely by our willpower and shut the feeling brain up; then congratulate this abuse of emotions and call it self-control.

Our Brain doesn’t work that way. Ironically, the feeling brain is the one behind the wheels, the adult. The thinking brain turns out to be the annoying kid on the passenger’s seat. This is because our actions are entirely driven by emotions. Joy, anger, fear, anxiousness, all move our bodies into actions. This is how addictions are made. Everyone knows nicotine is bad for our bodies, why do they still smoke? It is not because they don’t know better, it is because they don’t feel better. Not only addiction is an emotional problem, in fact most problems are emotional instead of logical. Procrastination, laziness, under-achievement are all emotional problems.

We all have a false assumption that our feelings and emotions are often distractions and annoying thoughts in our head that stops us from getting real work done. The consciousness car analogy tells us that it is impossible to surpass feelings with “self-control”, but rather we should negotiate between the two brains. The better feeling brain communicates with the thinking brain, the more motivated we are in getting things done and living a good life.

### Student Project with IKEA ⏳

[IMAGE: images/kaden-yeung-december-newsletter_img3.jpg]
IKEA is a billion-dollar-company. In 2023, IKEA was valued at over $21 billion, making it the seventh most valuable retail brand in the world. Imagine being told at 16-year-old,  that IKEA wants you to improve their customer experiences.

For the last two weeks, I have been working on a project with IKEA to revolutionize their customer experiences. What could IKEA do to provide the best customer experiences for customers across the globe? This is an opportunity to work on A Real World Problem. In TKS, a challenge season occurs during every fall. TKS collaborates with IKEA this fall, and challenge all innovates to a business case study competition. My buddies and I committed to this 2-week sprint of hustling, treated it as if we were building our own company, and learnt a lot about how to run a business. I have been more involved in a real-world commercial environment. For example, my team visited the IKEA store during the week. We interviewed many employees on inquiries they faced with customers and how they think the company can improve; we also interviewed the customers, we dived deeply into their needs when it comes to affordable furnitures. Not only did we got valuable first-handed insights from people who understand the industry, we grew understanding and experiences on reaching out and asking the right questions. We learnt to put together a high-standard slide-deck, in particular, the executive summary section serves the purpose of a hook that attracts investors’ intention.
