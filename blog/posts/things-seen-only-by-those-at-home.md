# Things seen only by those at home with Nature and its works.

_If I die by next morning, the wheel of time goes on, nature goes on._

Source: https://kadeny.substack.com/p/things-seen-only-by-those-at-home

---

Human species were out of the picture one quiet evening when I sat in silence at the porch.

Whispers of the rustling leaves and distant chirping drew me into the nature’s wonder. The wonder not simply stemmed from “pleasing melodies”, but the realization that this reality is isolated from us. We live in coherence with reality, yet they do not participate in human discourse.

Nature, within the context of philosophy, does not represent woods and forests and singing of sparrows, it is another word for our reality. It exists as a form of power, far away from our daily business, yet humans developed a hobby in defining certain parts of our perceived reality with “meanings”. Truth be told, they are meaningless, but that is a future topic of discussion. In the next few minutes of our time I want to shed lights on the beauty of nature, our reality.

Marcus Aurelius says, “we should remember that even Nature’s inadvertence has its own charm, its own attractiveness.”

Our reality operates under its own rules, independent from human species; though they don’t give a shit, its peculiar beauty is everywhere, from the loveliness of children to the distinct beauty in old age in men and women.

“The way loaves of bread split open on top in the oven”, “stalks of wheat bending under their own weight”, “flecks of foam on the boar’s mouth”.

We often overlook beauty of our reality. What is beauty anyways? Our reality does its thing regardless of our existences, beauty is simply defined by our perception.

“If you look at them in isolation there’s nothing beautiful about them, and yet by supplementing nature they enrich it and draw us in.”

Just as nature drew me in that one quiet evening.

“Anyone with a feeling for nature, with a deeper sensitivity, will find it all gives pleasure. Even what seems inadvertent.”

Realize that our reality is not evil, at its most, inadvertent yet beautiful. And being able to take pleasure in being part of our reality is one of the best ways to live. In Marcus’ own words: living in accordance with nature.

If I die by next morning, the wheel of time goes on, nature goes on.

But while I am alive, the blessing of nature carries.

Meditations, Book 3.
