# Books & Podcasts ⏰

I came across invaluable content that will change the trajectory of my life.

- The Knowledge Project with Naval Ravikant NAVAL Ravikant explained big ideas in simple words. In this podcast, he reflected on life and success, and finding true happiness at the moment. He introduced a Buddhist concept, “The Monkey Mind”, which is a silly voice in our head. Have you ever been over-worried about a problem that doesn’t exist? Or fantasize about a situation that will never come true? Most people have a voice in their head, it tells them what to do, how to think, and how to react; they rely heavily on this “Monkey Mind”, and allow the it to take over themselves entirely . Naval described the “Monkey Mind” as a distracting mind, where made-up thoughts wander around our head and become uncontrollable, cause unnecessary stress and anxiety. In order to take back full control of ourselves, Naval said that we must reflect back on our thoughts and identify the “Monkey Mind”. The ability to notice our own thoughts is an incredibly powerful tool, which is the key of Naval’s lesson, to distinguish between “Monkey Mind”(what we think) versus the facts (what actually happened). No matter how much we think about something, the enduring facts are what truly matters, therefore, Naval warns to stop living in imagination and self-doubt, but rather to live with the uncertainty and build a successful future.
- “Lifespan: Why We Age and Why We don’t Have To” by David A. Sinclair
I never thought I’d be so obsessed with BIOLOGY, but how could I not? Our bodies are literally biology. I came across a new area of interest when I read the book. Dr. David Sinclair challenges the common notions of aging and dying. Everybody believes aging is an inevitable change, but is it? Dr. Sinclair’s journey on researching the cause of aging and reverse aging is a game-changer for humanity.

#### Potato Gun - Hackathon 💻

YUP, you’ve heard it right. I built a Potato Gun at a Hackathon. It was my first ever Hackathon at TKS (The Knowledge Society). There was only one goal at the Hackathon, was to BUILD. I gathered a squad, we decided to follow our interest and curiosity and build something fun - a POTATO GUN. We did not exactly build a potato gun, but instead we built a Q-tip cannon, based on a how a potato gun works. We started from scratch and an inspiration from a Youtube shorts clip. Within the first hour, we gathered all materials we needed from a dollar store; electrical wires from a racket zapper, gun barrel from a pen, fuel from hair spray, gun body from a pill can…

We put all the pieces together, and after an afternoon of countless trials and disappointing errors, an improvised weapon came to life…

# Struggles 🍯


#### Sleep Schedule 🌃

MILLIONS of people ignore the importance of sufficient sleep, especially teenagers. As a 16-year old student, I am totally guilty of being sleep-deprived. Recently, I have noticed that my energy level has drastically decreased. An average teenager needs 8-10 hours of sleep; currently averaging 4 hours of asleep each night, something has to change. There are two mental approaches to organize and incentivize a healthy sleeping schedule.

- Try to go to bed as early as possible
- Get up early in the morning and complete important tasks
While most people follow the first approach, they often fail: thinking about going to bed early, but ended up being caught between tasks and useless distractions. As they may ask, “Why should we sleep early?” Although most people recognize the importance of sufficient sleep, it never seemed to be the priority at the moment, hence we tend to procrastinate sleeping.

The second approach is unconventional. Getting up even earlier while being sleep-deprived sounds like a bad idea. However, having an important task planned in the morning motivates our brains to get rest. Our brain actively reminds us to go to bed since something has to be done early in the morning. For example, build the habit of working out early in the morning. In order to workout early in the morning, we force ourselves to sleep early at night. This unconventional approach to sleeping incentivizes our brains to go to sleep.
