# finishing high-school, starting an agency, and teaching my 13-year old brother how to code.

_2026, first half._

Source: https://kadeny.substack.com/p/finishing-high-school-starting-an

---

Hi friends! Kaden here.

I’m finishing high-school in June. And I’m committed to Queen’s University Engineering in September.

It gives me mixed emotions. On one hand, I’m excited for a new environment; on another hand, I wish I’d stay to hang around the people I love and do the things i love, in Vancouver.

So when time clicks and the scaffolding clears away, I started to find out quickly what and who matters the most. After everything I’ve done in the article, I somehow circled back to the same thought.

This article is the highlight of everything that happened in the first five months (basically the first half).


### HABs + Cerebras

Earlier in the year I was deep in research on harmful algal blooms, specifically how they shut down desalination plants and what better mitigation can be done. The problem I was working on was auto-dosing of specific coagulants (or powdered activated carbon) at intake systems, replacing manual approach that barely moves the needle on plants downtime. We were in contact with an XPrize executive about implementation.

[IMAGE: images/finishing-high-school-starting-an_img1.png]

Around the same time, I built out a drug discovery lead identification dashbaord for a Cerebras Hackathon in 48 hours. The idea was parallel agent orchestration: running multiple AI agents simultaneously to process drug compounds. Cerebras’ inference speed was the key factor that made the architecture viable. I built the entire dashboard in 2 days with after-school hours. It was a tough hackathon, but it brought me to a fire-chat with the CTO at Cerebras (worthwhile).

### Building a photo booth.

This one started as a mentor challenge: earn $1000 in four weeks, and slowly it became something entirely differently.

Beginning of the challenge, I landed on building a photo booth to rent out for events. I already have an IPad and access to a woodwork shop so I thought it’s a convenient project.

I started researching cameras, sketching out the enclosure, connecting the hardware. At some point, I realized I was more excited about this than I had ever been with all of my technical projects on my resume. I believe it was because nobody gave me a standard and I’m allowed to make the photo-booth however I want. It gave “passion project” a real definition.

### Victory Velocity — GEO + ChatGPT Ads Agency

This is what I’m building right now.

The spark came from a friend who built a service around GEO— Generative Engine Optimizaition. Shout out to Noah Barbaros!

GEO is about helping business show up in AI searches and chats. He predicted the early pattern in GEO and provided optimization services to e-commerce based on his thesis. I wanted to understand how he studied the pattern, and through one of our chats he mentioned the hidden opportunities in a rising trend — ChatGPT Ads.

ChatGPT Ads opened access for any companies in May (just two weeks ago). The entry bar was reduced tremendously, including removing a $50k minimum spend threshold. But most businesses I talk to have never heard about any of this, let alone the ability to set up their brands on ChatGPT ads.

So there was GEO, now a new layer—ChatGPT ads is released. Our agency, Victory Velocity, is positioned to do both for our clients. We are now servicing our first 2 free clients. Our goal is to develop real success case studies in the still early ChatGPT ads wave before the market gets crowded. The timing window is narrow, but my co-founder and I are pouring our hearts and souls into this agency. I believe that our agency will be positioned in the top 5% in the ChatGPT ads agency market. Although only time can prove, once metrics roll out in the next 2-3 weeks.

Anyways, more to come about our agency.

### Side projects & other things

I taught my 13- year old brother vibe-coding. At first, he was against it. But he was a big fan of music production, and I convinced him that we can build a “DJ-mixing app” in less than 3 hours. So we made it.

RemixR is a browser-based DJ app (using Replit Agent, Tone.js, and React). A 3x3 checkpoint remixer on the left, drum pads on the right, vinyl scratch, two-deck tab switching. I added the dark and purple touch to make it look professional (although my brother was against it).

Vibe-coding had never been so much fun, especially when I get it build something with my little brother and watching his face lit up every once a while.

Last month, I built a “write someone/ or yourself a letter” app. The app lets me write/ journal in the form of writing someone a letter. I’m sure the past me had some relief after writing the letter, maybe I’ll even show the person one day.

Oh one more thing — I set a goal to gain 15 pounds in two months, only to realize that bulking is more difficult than it seems, especially being one of the skinner kids throughout my life. I know exactly what the problem is — eating. It seems like no matter how much I stuff my mouth with food, it simply isn’t enough. My weight always goes up throughout the day and falls back to default the next morning.

So I decided to eat even more and track calories in my food as much as possible. My new goal: 160lbs by the end of summer.


### Most 18 year olds aren’t ready to be 18.

[IMAGE: images/finishing-high-school-starting-an_img8.jpg]
And I can’t speak for everyone, but those closest to me can relate.

For most people, the months before moving out for university feels scary mixed with excitement. But for those holding on to people they love, it’s daunting.  Because stepping into uncertainty is one thing, but what about the people we leave behind?

I’m done with high-school in weeks.

No matter what projects I’ve built, metrics I’ve chased, the “on-paper” success;

I struggle to keep whom I really love.

Thank you and see you next month.
