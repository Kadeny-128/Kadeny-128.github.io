## How to fight wildfire in 2025?


#### I’m building this right now.

Every year, wildfires destroy over seven million acres in North America and cause more than $50 billion in damages, that is the cost of 50 of the world’s biggest cruise ships. It’s commonly believed it is the human behaviours that ignite the wildfires, and therefore unpredictable.

The truth is, it is not always the sparks that cause wildfires, it is the fuels. The forest fuels ignite quickly and spread fire rapidly. It is a crucial factor of wildfires.

Yet weather stations only collect data high up in the sky, no ground-based data on fuel conditions are detected. It makes no sense.

I have a plan, to make ground-based sensors that watches fuel condition, sends data to cloud for fire risk scores. By doing this, I detect likelihood of wildfires before they start, so wildfire services know exactly where and how to prevent them.

[IMAGE: images/17-and-building-chapter-1_img2.png]
There, that is the vision of our company, Prevention > Execution.

I made a pitch, a 1-pager and a landing page on v0 - our website!

I had an opportunity to pitch to Jay Crone (VC at Deloitte) and Anna Baird (runs Google AI).


#### They didn’t ban the idea, and I took that as a WIN.

Moving forward I will build sensor prototypes and connect with BC Wildfire Services.

Lucky for me, my mentor worked directly with them on a similar project, having

someone or a community to help me stay strong.

The truth is, I don’t know if it works yet, but I’ll share more tech depth in the future.

#### In the last week I dug into authenticity issues.

“Am I building for the sake of building?”

I have been following the Silicon Valley myth - that building = progress.

Sometimes I feel like digging a hole without knowing what to look for.

So many times my brain tells me ‘this isn’t it!!!”,

I ignored it because I thought I should be building regardless.

“Am I networking for the sake of networking?”

Sometimes I notice a pattern in meetings -

I’m too focused on what I want to get from these interactions that I forgot to show up as someone who’s genuinely curious and prepared to engage.

I catch myself trying to impress, even treating it like performance reviews instead of genuine connections. Just another person trying to prove his worth.

The truth is -  it’s difficult to hold back the urge to impress,

being 17 often feels like I have to prove my worth.

The more I catch myself with these patterns, the better clarity gets to me.


#### Claude hit me really hard with this quote:

“you're not actually trying to build a startup. You're trying to build a version of yourself that feels worthy of respect and opportunity. The products, the meetings, the networking - they're all elaborate scaffolding around this core project of becoming someone you can be proud of.”

I believe deep down, that might be true.

But who knows?

At the end of the day, I’m not going to kill an opportunity because Claude says so.

I will experiment and figure it out myself.


## Free-write!


#### About a week ago, I started free-write.

It is a Mac APP as simple as a blank page and a timer, and some fonts to mix around with - like google doc for journaling, but much simpler.

It told me to write whatever pops into your head, but there are a few rules once the timer starts:

[IMAGE: images/17-and-building-chapter-1_img4.png]
- no backspaces

- No fixing spelling

- Little 5–10s breaks are fine, but try to not stop typing

- No need to stay on the topic you started with — let your mind wander

- No judgment — trust your mind!

I realized free-write is not just a word doc, it is a practice to let your consciousness flow. The journals were full of collections of hidden thoughts. I let all those thoughts out, and it felt good. I shot my journal to Claude. The reflections usually would blow my mind again.

If you ever find yourself trapped in weird thoughts or confused by situations, “freewrite” it out.

I use free-write religiously on a daily basis, it’s my comfort app for bad thoughts that enter my brain.
